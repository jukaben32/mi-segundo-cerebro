## Insight 1 — Shipping costs spiked 244.3% in March and are eroding gross margin

Shipping & Freight rose from $2,010.00 in February to $6,920.45 in March — a $4,910.45 increase against a revenue gain of only $13,670.00. As a share of revenue, shipping went from 2.6% in February to 7.6% in March.

This is the primary driver of gross margin compression: February's gross margin was 75.3%; March fell back to 66.5% — nearly identical to January's 67.0%, erasing February's gains entirely.

| Month | Shipping Cost | Revenue | Shipping as % of Revenue |
|-------|--------------|---------|--------------------------|
| January | $1,830.00 | $59,200.00 | 3.1% |
| February | $2,010.00 | $76,900.00 | 2.6% |
| March | $6,920.45 | $90,570.00 | 7.6% |

The magnitude of this increase relative to both prior months and revenue growth warrants immediate investigation. Possible causes include a new freight contract, higher shipment volumes, return processing costs, or misclassified expenses.

## Insight 2 — Marketing spend more than doubled in March with diminishing revenue return

Marketing & Advertising rose from $1,370.00 in February to $3,720.00 in March — a 171.5% increase. Revenue grew only 17.8% over the same period. Marketing as a share of revenue moved from 1.8% to 4.1%.

| Month | Marketing Spend | Revenue Growth |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Jan → Feb | +$90.00 (+7.0%) | +$17,700.00 (+29.9%) |
| Feb → Mar | +$2,350.00 (+171.5%) | +$13,670.00 (+17.8%) |

In January and February, modest marketing spend coincided with stronger revenue growth. The March increase delivered a smaller proportional return. This does not confirm causation, but the pattern warrants scrutiny — particularly whether the higher spend is producing sustainable channel growth or is front-loading customer acquisition costs.

## Insight 3 — Net income declined 8.9% in March despite record revenue

March posted the highest revenue of the quarter ($90,570.00) but net income fell from $21,420.60 to $19,522.86 — a drop of $1,897.74. Net margin contracted from 27.9% to 21.6%.

The cause is cost growth outpacing revenue growth across all categories:

| | Feb → Mar Change |
|--|-----------------|
| Revenue | +17.8% |
| Total COGS | +59.8% |
| Total Operating Expenses | +11.5% |

This is not a revenue problem — it is a cost control problem concentrated in March. The shipping spike (Insight 1) and marketing increase (Insight 2) account for the bulk of the gap. If both normalize in April, margins should recover. If they persist, the business will report lower net income on higher revenue, a structural margin compression trend.

## Insight 4 — Amazon dependency (78.5% of Q1 revenue) is a concentrated platform risk

Amazon generated $177,920.00 of the $226,670.00 in Q1 revenue — 78.5%. eBay contributed 11.8% ($26,650.00) and Shopify Direct 9.7% ($22,100.00).

| Channel | Q1 Revenue | Share | MoM Growth (Feb→Mar) |
|---------|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| Amazon | $177,920.00 | 78.5% | +12.6% |
| eBay | $26,650.00 | 11.8% | +22.4% |
| Shopify Direct | $22,100.00 | 9.7% | +62.9% |

Any disruption to the Amazon channel — policy changes, account suspension, fee increases, or algorithm shifts — would immediately threaten the majority of revenue. This concentration is not unusual for e-commerce businesses at this stage, but the exposure is significant.

On a positive note, Shopify Direct is the fastest-growing channel at +62.9% Feb→Mar (from $6,200 to $10,100). At this growth rate, it is beginning to close the gap with eBay and warrants active investment.

## Insight 5 — High fixed cost base creates significant margin vulnerability in low-revenue months

January's net margin of 6.9% ($4,080.80 net income on $59,200.00 revenue) reveals how exposed the business is when revenue dips. Payroll alone was $29,600.00 in January — 50.0% of total revenue. Adding rent and insurance, fixed costs totaled approximately $33,360.00, or 56.4% of January revenue.

At January's revenue level, a 10% revenue decline ($5,920) would have reduced net income by 145% — pushing the month to a net loss.

| Fixed Cost Item | Q1 Monthly Average | % of January Revenue |
|-----------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| Payroll | $30,200.00 | 51.0% |
| Rent & Facilities | $3,200.00 | 5.4% |
| Insurance | $560.00 | 0.9% |
| **Total Fixed** | **$33,960.00** | **57.4%** |

February and March recovered due to revenue growth, not cost reduction. The business has no meaningful fixed cost flexibility; protection against a weak revenue month requires either a cash reserve buffer or a plan to reduce variable costs rapidly when needed.
